Canadian Stamp Series

For this piece I was tasked with creating a set of 4 stamps in a cohesive series based on any topic regarding Canada. I decided that embroidering flowers would look striking as a stamp so I did research on provincial/ territorial flowers and I chose 4 that would transfer well to the medium of embroidery. 

I chose British Columbia's Pacific dogwood, Yukon's fireweed, Quebec's iris versicolor, and Saskatchewan's red lily. I also researched embroidery techniques such as French knots to achieve a more detailed and textured look to better represent elements of flowers such as the Fireweed's small round petals. 

I painted cotton fabric in contrasting or compliementary colours of the flowers so they would stand out against their background, and then I used an embroidery hoop and embroidery floss to create the flowers. 

I added the text overtop digitally to give context to what the flowers are and where they come from.
